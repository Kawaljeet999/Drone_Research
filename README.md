## 1. Introduction
With the rapid growth of drone (UAV) usage, concerns related to **security, privacy, and illegal surveillance** have increased significantly. Drone Forensics is a research domain that focuses on **analyzing drone-related data to identify drones, understand their behavior, and support post-incident investigation**.

This research studies how **machine learning and data analysis techniques** can be applied to drone telemetry, signal, and behavior data to assist forensic and security applications.

---

## 2. Research Problem
Most existing drone detection systems depend on **hardware-based solutions** such as radar, RF scanners, or acoustic sensors. These systems are often:
- Costly  
- Environment-dependent  
- Difficult to scale  

This research investigates whether **software-based, data-driven forensic methods** can provide:
- Drone identification  
- Behavioral analysis  
- Anomaly and intrusion detection  

---

## 3. Research Objectives
The objectives of this research are:
- To study drone-related datasets from a forensic perspective  
- To extract meaningful features from drone data  
- To apply machine learning models for drone identification  
- To analyze suspicious or abnormal drone behavior  

---

## 4. Datasets Considered
This research framework supports multiple public and research datasets, including:

1. **Drone RF Signal Dataset**  
   RF fingerprints used for identifying drone models.

2. **UAV Flight Telemetry Dataset**  
   GPS coordinates, altitude, speed, and trajectory data.

3. **Drone Acoustic Dataset**  
   Sound signatures generated by drone propellers.

4. **Radar-Based Drone Dataset**  
   Radar reflections for aerial object detection.

5. **Synthetic Drone Intrusion Dataset**  
   Simulated datasets representing unauthorized drone activity.

## 5. Research Methodology
The research follows a structured pipeline:

1. Data understanding and inspection  
2. Data preprocessing and normalization  
3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
4. Feature engineering  
5. Machine learning model training  
6. Evaluation and forensic interpretation

These methods are selected for **interpretability**, which is essential in forensic analysis.
